## Organization Profile: The Arkham Historical Society

**Organization ID:** ORG-002  
**Name:** The Arkham Historical Society  
**Type:** Historical Organization  
**Relationship:** Information Sharing Agreement  
**Established:** 1852  
**Location:** Arkham, Massachusetts

## Overview

The Arkham Historical Society is a non-profit organization dedicated to preserving and sharing the history of Arkham and the surrounding region. The society maintains an extensive archive of local historical documents, photographs, and artifacts dating back to the 17th century.

## Areas of Collaboration

- Historical context for anomaly investigations
- Access to municipal records and archives
- Local folklore and oral history documentation
- Historical site identification and preservation
- Community liaison services

## Key Contacts

- **Margaret Winters:** Executive Director
- **Thomas Bradley:** Chief Archivist
- **Eleanor Cross:** Curator of Collections

## Notable Resources

- Pre-1800 Arkham municipal records
- Historical maps and property records
- Photographic archive (1850-present)
- Oral history collection
- Maritime history records

## Partnership Agreement

Information sharing agreement established in 1993. The society provides historical context and research support while Lovecraft Inc. offers preservation funding and occasional consultation on historical mysteries.

## Security Clearance

No formal clearance. Information shared is carefully vetted to exclude classified details. The society's staff are not aware of the true nature of our operations.

## Notes

The society has been particularly helpful in tracking historical patterns that correlate with anomaly manifestations. Their oral history collection has provided several leads on previously undocumented anomalies. Quarterly liaison meetings are held to discuss areas of mutual interest.

## Special Collections

The society holds several collections of particular interest:
- The Whateley Family Papers
- Innsmouth Maritime Records (1800-1930)
- Arkham Sanitarium Records (1880-1920)
- Early Colonial Settlement Documents
